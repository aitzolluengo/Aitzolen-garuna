# Inversions (B2)

Las inversions (inversiones) son una característica de la gramática inglesa que se utiliza para dar énfasis o crear un estilo más formal, dramático o literario. Consisten en cambiar el orden normal de sujeto-verbo por verbo-sujeto, similar a una pregunta, pero en una oración afirmativa.

## Usos Comunes de las Inversions en B2

Las inversiones se utilizan principalmente con adverbios o expresiones con significado negativo o restrictivo al inicio de la frase.

### 1. Con Adverbios Negativos o Restrictivos

Cuando los siguientes adverbios o frases adverbiales se colocan al inicio de la oración, se produce una inversión.

*   **Never (before), Rarely, Seldom, Hardly (ever), Scarcely (ever), Barely (ever):**
    *   *Never before have I seen such a beautiful sunset.* (Normal: *I have never seen such a beautiful sunset before.*)
    *   *Hardly had she left the house when it started to rain.* (Normal: *She had hardly left the house when it started to rain.*)
*   **No sooner... than:**
    *   *No sooner had I arrived than the party started.* (Normal: *I had no sooner arrived than the party started.*)
*   **Only after, Only then, Only when, Only by (doing something):**
    *   *Only after finishing the work did he go home.* (Normal: *He went home only after finishing the work.*)
    *   *Only by working together can we solve this problem.* (Normal: *We can only solve this problem by working together.*)
*   **Little:** Cuando significa "no mucho" o "apenas".
    *   *Little did I know that I would never see her again.* (Normal: *I little knew that I would never see her again.*)
*   **Not only... but also:**
    *   *Not only did he lose his job, but he also lost his house.* (Normal: *He not only lost his job, but he also lost his house.*)
*   **Under no circumstances / On no account / In no way:**
    *   *Under no circumstances are you to open that door.* (Normal: *You are not to open that door under any circumstances.*)

### 2. Estructura de la Inversion

La estructura es como la de una pregunta:

*   **Auxiliar + Sujeto + Verbo Principal**
    *   *Never **have I seen**...* (Present Perfect)
    *   *Hardly **had she left**...* (Past Perfect)
    *   *Only after finishing **did he go**...* (Past Simple)
    *   *Little **do I know**...* (Present Simple)
